Bioscience & Food Security Application

Modeling MR297 Rice Growth Rates

Visualizing how Derivatives ($f'(x)$) optimize agricultural inputs and predict yield timelines in Peninsular Malaysia.

The Field Scenario

To maximize national **Food Security** and move closer to self-sufficiency targets, agronomists model the dry biomass accumulation of elite local rice varieties like **MR297** (developed by MARDI).

By pinpointing exactly when the growth acceleration shifts, farm managers can apply nitrogen fertilizers at the precise physiological window when the crop demands it most, preventing nutrient run-off and optimizing yields.

The Mathematics

Growth Function $f(x)$
f(x) = L / (1 + e^(-k * (x - x0)))

A Logistic Model where **L = 150g** (maximum crop biomass capacity), **k = 0.08** (growth rate coefficient), and **x0 = 55** (inflection point day).

The First Derivative $f'(x)$
f'(x) = k * f(x) * (1 - f(x)/L)

Derived using the Chain Rule. It outputs the *instantaneous growth rate* (biomass accumulation speed) at exactly day $x$.
